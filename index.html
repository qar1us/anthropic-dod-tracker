Background

The contract that started it all

In early 2026, Anthropic formalized a partnership with the Department of Defense, allowing military use of its AI systems. For a company that had built its identity around AI safety and responsible deployment, the move landed like a grenade.

Jan 14, 2026
Anthropic–DOD contract surfaces. Details of a multi-year agreement emerge via reporting. Anthropic confirms the partnership.
Jan 16, 2026
First employee letter circulates. Dozens of Anthropic researchers and engineers sign an internal letter questioning the decision.
Jan 19, 2026
Open letter published. 200+ AI researchers and advocates publish a public statement demanding Anthropic rescind or narrow the contract.
Feb 3, 2026
Congressional inquiry. House Science Committee requests briefing on AI contracts with military agencies.
Mar 25, 2026
Tracking continues. 47 entities now on record. The silence of major players grows louder.
Sector 01

Big Tech speaks — unevenly

Silicon Valley's response has been fractured. Google and Microsoft, both with their own defense contracts, have stayed largely quiet. OpenAI and Meta have made statements. Smaller players have been more vocal.
